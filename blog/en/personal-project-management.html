Why Trello doesn't work for personal projects

Trello was built to coordinate teams with visual workflows. Cards in columns (To Do → In Progress → Done) make sense when multiple people move cards and need to see the team's status at a glance.

When you use it alone, the model breaks. Cards have no hierarchy — you can't have real subtasks inside a task. There's no connection between your projects and your notes. There are no smart filters. And there's definitely no AI that understands your content.

For personal projects you need capture speed, natural hierarchy, and context. That's exactly what Fromly offers.

Fromly's hierarchy model

In Fromly, everything is a node. A project is a node. Its phases are sub-nodes. The tasks in each phase are sub-nodes of sub-nodes. There's no depth limit — you can go as deep as you need.

This hierarchy gives you something Trello can't: permanent context. When you see a task, you always know which project it belongs to, which phase it's in, and what surrounds it. No tags, no cross-references needed.

Nested tasks: unlimited subtasks

In Trello, subtasks are a clunky add-on — a checklist inside a card that doesn't have the same functionality as a real task. In Fromly, a subtask is exactly like a normal task but nested under another one.

This means you can:

  • Nest tasks as deep as you need
  • See the status of subtasks from the parent task
  • Filter pending subtasks without losing project context
  • Assign dates to tasks and subtasks independently
  • Create notes inside a task to document the work

💡 Hierarchy tip: Use Tab to make any node a child of the node above it. Shift+Tab moves it up a level. Reorganizing entire projects takes seconds.

See only what matters at each moment

The trap of managing multiple projects is seeing too much at once. When you open Trello with five boards, you have to mentally zoom in to find what needs doing today.

In Fromly, filters solve this. Click "Pending" in the sidebar and you see only your pending tasks from all projects, sorted by date with their project context visible. Click "This week" and you see only what's due this week.

You can create your own saved filters: "Overdue tasks", "Web project this week", "Unprocessed ideas". Each is a one-click shortcut in the sidebar.

Create complete projects with AI in seconds

Imagine you need to manage a new product launch. In Trello it would take 15–20 minutes to create the board, lists, cards, and configure members. In Fromly, open Magic AI and write:

"Create a project called 'Q3 Product Launch' with phases: market research, design, development, marketing, and launch. In each phase add a pending task to define objectives."

In 5 seconds you have the complete structure created with a single message.

The daily workflow

With Fromly, the personal project workflow is simple:

  1. Morning: press H to see today's note. Your tasks for the day are immediately visible.
  2. During the day: work on the tasks of the current project. Navigate to the project node for full context.
  3. New task: press N to add a task directly to the node you're in, or use Magic AI to add it to the right project.
  4. Review: use "This week" or "Pending" to see the global status of all projects.
  5. End of day: mark completed tasks. Status is recorded automatically.
