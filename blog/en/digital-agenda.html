The real advantage of paper planners

Millions of people still use paper planners in 2026, despite having smartphones and computers. It's not nostalgia — it's because a paper planner does something digital apps don't do well: it puts everything in the same place.

In a physical planner, Tuesday's page has the day's events, space for meeting notes, and a task list, all in one glance. In the digital world, that fragments into three separate apps that don't talk to each other.

Why digital agendas fall short

Classic digital agendas — Google Calendar, Apple Calendar — are excellent for managing events. But they're not a complete productivity system. Notes live in a different app. Tasks live in another. The result is fragmented information and lost context.

When you have a client meeting, the event is in Google Calendar, the prep notes are in Notion, the action items are in Todoist, and the follow-up email is in Gmail. None of these apps knows about the others. You become the manual connector — and that carries a huge cognitive cost.

How Fromly combines the best of both worlds

Fromly takes the core idea of the paper planner — everything in the same place — and brings it digital with capabilities paper can't match:

  • Automatic daily note — The equivalent of your planner's daily page, with automatic dating and no page-turning
  • Tasks with context — Tasks aren't a loose list; they're nested under the day, project, or meeting that originated them
  • Calendar events — Google Calendar integrated: your day's events appear in the daily note
  • Instant search — Find any note or decision from the past in seconds
  • Sync — Mac, web, and iPhone with the same information in real time

Google Calendar integrated: your events in context

The Google Calendar integration is two-way. Your events appear in Fromly's planner and in the daily note. If you create an event in Fromly, it shows up in Google Calendar.

This means when you start your workday and open Fromly, you see at a glance: what you need to do today (pending tasks), who you're meeting with (GCal events), and what's left from yesterday (overdue tasks).

📅 Agenda tip: Press P to open the planner alongside your daily note. You get a full view of your commitments next to your notes and tasks space — exactly like a paper planner, but digital.

Your daily routine with Fromly as your agenda

The transition from paper planner to Fromly is smooth if you follow this routine for the first few weeks:

  1. Morning: open Fromly and press H. The daily note already exists with your calendar events.
  2. Before each meeting: navigate to the meeting node (or create it) and add preparation context.
  3. During the meeting: take notes directly in the meeting node — tasks with Cmd+Enter, notes as plain text.
  4. After the meeting: 5 minutes to turn notes into concrete tasks with dates.
  5. End of day: mark completed tasks as done. A quick glance at "Pending" to see what's left.
