What a second brain is and why you need one

The concept of a "second brain" or Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) has been circulating in productivity circles for years. The idea is simple: your biological brain isn't built to store information, it's built to process it. When you try to simultaneously remember passwords, dates, book titles, task lists, and important conversations, performance drops.

The solution is to offload that cognitive burden to a digital system. A second brain is that system: it captures everything that comes in, organizes it with structure, and surfaces it when you need it.

The problem with most note apps is that they require too much configuration to be useful. Notion has an enormous learning curve. Obsidian requires plugins and manual management. Roam Research is powerful but chaotic to start with.

From takes a different approach: hierarchical tree with integrated AI. The structure is natural — the same way you think — and the AI helps you capture and retrieve without effort.

The base structure: 5 folders that change everything

You don't need a perfect structure to start. You need one that's good enough to maintain without effort. Here's what we recommend:

  • Active projects — Everything with a concrete outcome and an end date
  • Learning — Books, courses, podcasts, notes, study material
  • Resources — Permanent references: articles, documents, templates
  • Ideas — Undefined thoughts that could become projects
  • Archive — Completed projects, old references, historical context

This structure, known as PARA (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archive), was popularized by Tiago Forte. In From you can set it up in under a minute using Magic AI.

💡 Tip: Open Magic AI with the sparkle button in the bottom right corner, write "Create a Second Brain note with subnodes: Active projects, Learning, Resources, Ideas, and Archive" and confirm. Done in 10 seconds.

Quick capture: the key to the system

A second brain only works if capture is fast. If you have to think about where to save each thing, the system gets abandoned within a week. In From, capture has three modes depending on context:

Daily mode

Every day From automatically creates today's note. Press H to go to it from anywhere. Everything you write here is recorded with a date. At the end of the day you can move the relevant stuff to its permanent home — or leave it there, because the search filter will find it either way.

Project mode

When you're working on a specific project, navigate to that node and create subnodes directly. The hierarchy gives you automatic context: you can see which project each note belongs to without needing tags.

Voice mode

Hold R in the AI chat and speak. From transcribes and adds the node directly. For ideas that surface while walking, driving, or exercising.

Search and retrieval: find anything in seconds

A useless second brain is one where you save things but can't find them. From solves this with filters that understand natural language.

Press Cmd+F and write what you're looking for. You can combine criteria in plain language: "meeting notes from last week", "pending tasks in the web project", "everything related to learning".

The most-used filters can be saved as panels in the sidebar. Out of the box there's "Pending" and "This week". You can add your own: "Learning this week", "Unprocessed ideas", "Active projects with overdue tasks".

AI as your second brain assistant

The difference between From and a classic notes app is that the AI knows your content. It's not a generic chatbot: it knows what's in your tree and can act on it.

Practical use cases for the AI-powered second brain:

  • Process meeting notes — "Extract the tasks from today's meeting and assign them to the relevant projects"
  • Summarize learning — "Summarize the key points from my notes on the book I'm reading"
  • Connect ideas — "Is there any connection between my marketing notes and my product notes?"
  • Plan — "What do I have pending this week and in which projects?"

🧠 Key point: From's AI doesn't make things up — it works with your real information. The more you use the system, the more useful the AI becomes because it has more context about you and your projects.
