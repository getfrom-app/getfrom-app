From in Raycast: create, search and open today's note without leaving

The From extension for Raycast lets you capture, search and open today's note in one keystroke — From's intelligence decides if it's a note, task or event.

From Blog · 5 junio 2026 · 5 min read

The context-switch tax is real

You are in the middle of a task. A thought appears — something you need to capture before it disappears. So you switch to From, navigate to today's note, type it, and come back. Ten seconds, maybe fifteen. Harmless in isolation. Multiplied across a day, it adds up to something that quietly drains your focus.

The From extension for Raycast eliminates that tax entirely. Open Raycast, type what you need, press Enter. Done. You never left your work.

What the Raycast extension does

The extension gives you three core commands, each designed to remove a specific type of friction:

  • Create node — capture a thought, task, or event without opening From
  • Search — find anything inside your From workspace by keyword
  • Open today's note — jump straight to today's daily note in one keystroke

These are not generic shortcuts. Each one connects directly to From's understanding of your content — so what gets created, found, or opened is exactly what you intended.

From's intelligence decides whether what you typed is a note, a task, or an event. You don't label it. You don't click a dropdown. You just write.

Create without thinking about type

Most capture tools make you decide upfront: is this a task? A note? An event? That micro-decision is friction. It interrupts the thought.

With From's Raycast extension, you type naturally. "Call the accountant tomorrow at 10" becomes an event. "Read the new pricing doc" becomes a task. "Thoughts on the Q3 structure" becomes a note. From reads what you wrote and places it correctly inside your workspace — in today's note by default, or wherever you configure it.

Everything captured through Raycast lands in today's note, organized automatically.

This matters because the moment of capture is the worst moment to make organizational decisions. You're mid-thought. From handles the classification so you can stay in the flow.

Search that understands your structure

From is an outliner. Your content lives in a hierarchy of nodes — notes nested inside contexts, tasks grouped by project, events tied to dates. Searching it from Raycast means you get results that respect that structure.

Type a keyword and From returns matching nodes with enough context to know which one you want. You can open a result directly in From, or act on it from Raycast. No loading the full app, no navigating the sidebar, no scrolling to find what you need.

The same contexts and shortcuts you navigate in From are accessible through Raycast search.

Today's note, one keystroke away

The daily note is where most From users anchor their day. It's where captures land, where morning planning happens, where quick thoughts get parked before they find a permanent home.

Opening it from Raycast means you assign a global shortcut — something like ⌥Space — and From's today note opens instantly, wherever you are on your Mac. No Dock click, no app switch, no navigation. The note is just there.

This is especially useful first thing in the morning and last thing before you close your laptop. Two moments where you want to interact with From quickly, without it becoming an event.

How to set it up

  1. Install the From extension from the Raycast Store (search "From")
  2. Connect your From account when prompted — it links to your existing workspace
  3. Assign keyboard shortcuts to the commands you'll use most
  4. Test the Create command with a simple capture — notice how From classifies it automatically

Setup takes under two minutes. The extension uses the same account and data as your From app — nothing is duplicated, nothing is out of sync. What you capture in Raycast appears immediately in From, and vice versa.

Who this is for

If you already use Raycast as your Mac command center, this extension turns From into a background layer that's always one shortcut away. You don't switch to From — you pull what you need from it without losing your place.

Power users will find the most value: people who capture dozens of things throughout the day, who need to search their notes constantly, and who treat today's note as a live working document rather than something they visit once. For them, the Raycast extension isn't a convenience — it's the primary interface.

From doesn't need Raycast to be useful. But with Raycast, it becomes invisible in the best possible way — present when you need it, out of the way when you don't.

The bottom line

The From extension for Raycast removes the last remaining reason to break your focus for a capture. It's fast, it understands what you write, and it keeps your workspace organized without asking you to do the organizing. If you use both tools, connecting them is the obvious next step.
