The device fragmentation problem

You start a note on Mac during a meeting. You want to review it on your iPhone while commuting. You get home and want to expand it in the browser because your computer isn't nearby. In most systems, this flow requires exports, manual syncing, or losing information along the way.

Device fragmentation is one of the biggest enemies of personal productivity. When your information isn't where you need it, you create duplicates, take screenshots, or simply abandon the system.

From solves this with a cloud architecture where the node tree is identical across all devices, always updated in real time.

From on each platform

Mac — The main hub

The Mac app is where most users spend the most time. It has the most complete feature set: keyboard shortcuts for everything, a visual planner with weekly view, native Google Calendar integration, Magic AI with voice capture, and access to the full tree.

The Mac app is native — it's not a web wrapper. That means it's fast, responds instantly, and integrates with the OS: notifications, Spotlight search, automatic dark mode.

iPhone — Capture on the go

The iPhone app is optimized for two use cases: quick idea capture on the move, and reviewing tasks and agenda when your Mac isn't available.

The iPhone widget lets you see your tasks for the day from the home screen without opening the app. Voice capture works with the iPhone microphone — an idea comes to you while walking, you say it out loud, and it appears in your tree within seconds.

Web — Universal access

If you're on someone else's computer, on an iPad, or on any device with a browser, you can access From at getfrom.app/app. Full functionality: node tree, filters, planner, and Magic AI.

How sync works

From's sync is real-time and automatic. There's no sync button, no manual exports. When you create a node on Mac, it appears on iPhone in under a second. When you mark a task as done on iPhone, it updates on Mac instantly.

The status bar at the bottom of From shows sync status: "Connected", "Syncing", or the time of the last automatic backup. You always know your information is safe.

🔒 Automatic backups: From automatically backs up your tree periodically. You can see when the last backup happened in the bottom status bar. If something goes wrong, your data is protected.

Cross-device workflows

These are the most common workflows From makes frictionless:

Mac to iPhone

You plan your week on Mac Monday morning. Tuesday you're commuting and review the day's tasks on iPhone from the widget. You mark a task done — it updates on Mac automatically.

iPhone to Mac

You have an idea while walking. You say it out loud into the iPhone app. You get to your Mac and the idea is already in your tree, ready to develop.

Without Mac — web only

You're at someone's place without your Mac. You open getfrom.app in the browser and have full access to all your information. You add a task, check notes from a meeting, review your weekly agenda.

Offline access

The Mac and iPhone apps have local cache: if you lose your connection, you can still view and edit your content. Changes made offline sync automatically when the connection is restored. Nothing is lost.
