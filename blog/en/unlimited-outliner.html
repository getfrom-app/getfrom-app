The unlimited outliner: nest, move and reorganize at the speed of thought

Workflowy speed, Notion power. Everything in one tree that never slows you down.

Your notes shouldn't slow you down

Most note-taking apps make you choose. You can have speed — a plain list that's fast but dumb — or you can have power — a structured system that understands context but bogs you down with clicks, properties, and database views. From refuses that trade-off.

From is an outliner that works at the speed of thought. You write, nest, and reorganize freely, and From understands what you're creating as you go — without you having to configure anything.

Everything lives in one tree. Nest anything inside anything else.

The real problem with most outliners

Workflowy-style tools are fast to type into but fragile the moment your notes grow. You end up with one massive dump that's impossible to navigate. Notion solves that with structure — but the moment you need to move a block, rename a database, or rethink your hierarchy, you're fighting the tool instead of thinking.

There's a hidden cost to every drag-and-drop that requires confirmation, every property you have to assign manually, every time you have to stop and decide: is this a task or a note?

From eliminates that cost entirely. You write naturally. From figures out the rest.

One tree. No limits on depth.

From's core is a single infinite tree. Every piece of information — a task, a meeting note, a half-formed idea, a project milestone — lives as a node. Nodes can be nested as deep as you need. You can collapse any branch. You can zoom in on any subtree and work there as if it's the whole document.

There are no pages. No separate databases. No "blocks" that behave differently depending on where you put them. A node is a node, and you can put it anywhere.

What makes From different is what happens as you write. If you prefix a line with a checkbox, From knows it's a task. If you write a date, From registers it as an event. If you link to a URL, From treats it as a resource. You don't declare types. From detects them.

From doesn't ask you to categorize your thoughts before you've finished having them. It waits, reads what you wrote, and understands.

Reorganizing without friction

The real test of any outliner is what happens when your structure changes — because it always does. Projects evolve. Priorities shift. What was a quick note becomes a full project. What was a top-level area becomes a sub-section of something else.

In From, reorganizing is trivial. You drag any node into any other node. You indent and outdent with keyboard shortcuts. You move entire branches — with all their children — in a single gesture. The tree reorders instantly, with no warnings, no migration dialogs, no broken links.

Right-click any node to move, convert, or act on it — without leaving your flow.

The contextual menu gives you every action you need — convert a note to a task, create a sibling below, duplicate, delete — without taking your hands off the keyboard or breaking your focus.

How this looks in practice

Here's a real workflow:

  1. You open From and start typing a brain dump from a meeting. No template, no structure — just lines.
  2. Some lines start with [ ]. From marks them as tasks automatically.
  3. You type a date next to one of them. From links it to your calendar view.
  4. You realize two of those tasks belong under a different project. You drag them there. Done.
  5. Later, you zoom into just that project and work inside it, without the rest of the tree in view.

Total time spent on structure: zero. You captured, organized, and planned — entirely by writing.

Filters that find, not search

When your tree grows, you need to surface what matters without digging. From's filter system lets you narrow the view to tasks, to specific dates, to nodes with certain tags or states — in one click. The filters don't open a new page. They collapse your existing tree to show only what matches.

It's the difference between searching for your notes and having your notes already organized so the right ones appear when you need them.

Keyboard-first, always

From is built for people who think faster than they click. Every action has a keyboard shortcut. Creating a node, indenting, moving up or down, collapsing a branch, opening the planner — all of it is reachable without a mouse. The sidebar gives you instant access to your most-used views and shortcuts, so navigation never interrupts the writing.

If you've ever used a tool and thought why am I fighting this? — From is the answer. It's designed to disappear and let you think.

Conclusion

From gives you the unlimited depth of a classic outliner with the intelligence to understand what you're writing as you write it. There's no setup, no schema to design, no tool to master — just a tree that grows with you and stays fast no matter how large it gets. Start typing. From handles the rest.
