How to take meeting notes you actually use afterwards

80% of meeting notes are never opened again. The solution isn't taking better notes — it's having a system that connects them to the tasks they generate.

Why meeting notes get abandoned

The problem isn't lack of motivation — it's lack of system. Meeting notes get abandoned for three reasons: they're disconnected from the tasks they generate, they're saved somewhere you don't revisit, and there's no review process to activate them.

In most note apps, a meeting note is just text. The tasks that come out of the meeting have to be manually copied to your task app. The meeting context is lost. The note ends up orphaned in a folder you never open.

From solves this because notes and tasks live in the same system. A task that comes up in a meeting can be nested directly under the note for that meeting, with its date and full context.

The meeting note structure that works

You don't need a perfect structure — you need one that's good enough to follow even when the meeting moves fast. Here's what we recommend:

  • Meeting name + date — As the node title (From creates it with the date automatically)
  • Context — One line on what the meeting is for and who's attending
  • Points discussed — Free notes during the meeting. No need to write in full sentences — just keywords
  • Decisions — What was clearly decided. This is the most valuable part
  • Action items — Each task with its owner and date if defined

How to take notes during the meeting

During the meeting, the goal is to capture without interrupting. Don't correct, don't format, don't search for the perfect place for each note. Just write.

In From, create the meeting node before it starts using Magic AI: "Create a meeting note 'Client X project review' with sections: context, points discussed, and action items". The structure is ready in 5 seconds.

💡 During the meeting: Use subnodes for each point. Write fast and don't filter. Enter for a new line, Tab to indent. When someone says "we need to do X", write the task and press Cmd+Enter to convert it to a task with a checkbox.

The 5-minute post-meeting process

The 5 minutes after a meeting are the most valuable. They're the only minutes where you have fresh context and can convert notes into concrete actions.

The process is simple:

  1. Identify the tasks — The ones you said "I'll do that" and the ones left open
  2. Convert to tasks — Press Cmd+Enter on each line that's an action
  3. Assign dates — For those with deadlines, add the date directly
  4. Link to the project — If the tasks belong to a specific project, move them there

With From, this process takes 3–5 minutes because everything is in the same system. No need to open another app, no need to copy text.

Finding old notes when you need them

The meeting was two weeks ago. Someone asks "what did we decide about X?". In most systems, that means searching through file folders or scrolling through an endless history.

In From, press Cmd+F and type "client X meeting" or the approximate date. Results appear with full context — including the tasks that meeting generated and whether they've been completed.

Using AI to process your notes

If the meeting was intense and your notes are messy, Magic AI can help you process them. Open the chat and ask:

  • "Extract the tasks from today's meeting notes and convert them to pending tasks"
  • "Summarize the key points from the client X meeting notes"
  • "Which tasks from this meeting are still incomplete?"

The AI works on your real notes, not generic information. The result is precise and actionable.
