Fromly vs Apple Notes: when the native app is no longer enough

Apple Notes is great until your notes become hundreds. Then you need something that understands you, not another folder.

Fromly Blog · June 5, 2026 · 5 min read

Apple Notes works. Until it doesn't.

For years, Apple Notes has been the obvious choice for iPhone and Mac users. It's built in, it's fast, it syncs without a hitch, and it costs nothing. For the first few weeks, it's perfect.

The problem shows up after months of use. When you have 200 notes, 40 lists tangled together, tasks you're not sure you finished, ideas you can't find, and meetings you jotted down somewhere but can't remember where. Apple Notes isn't a system. It's a drawer.

And drawers, once they're full, stop being useful.

The folder ceiling

Apple Notes gives you folders. You can sort your notes into categories: Work, Personal, Projects, Ideas. It seems like enough at first.

But the truth is thinking doesn't work in folders. A single meeting can produce a task, a resource, and an idea at the same time. Where does it go? Do you create three notes? Or one long note you'll never find again?

With Apple Notes, you end up doing one of two things: creating endless unstructured notes, or spending more time organizing than working. Neither one is real productivity.

The problem isn't that Apple Notes is bad. It's that it's built to store things, not to understand them. Once you need a system that knows what you write, a native notes app isn't enough anymore.

In Fromly, every note, task, or event lives in a clear context — no endless folders, no orphaned notes.

How to switch from Apple Notes

You don't need to migrate everything in one afternoon. You can start gradually:

  1. Start with your active tasks. Tell the chat what you have in Apple Notes, as-is. From the first moment you'll see how it detects and organizes them.
  2. Create one context per active project. No folders, no crossed tags. One named Area or Project, with everything related to it inside.
  3. Use today's note. Fromly generates a daily note automatically. Write what you're doing throughout the day there. By the end of the week, you'll have an effortless log.
  4. Let the chat help you. When you can't find something, ask it. When you need to summarize a batch of notes, ask it to. No complicated prompts required.

The chat knows your notes and answers with real context, not generic replies.

Apple Notes for the simple stuff, Fromly for what matters

This isn't about abandoning Apple Notes forever. If you need to jot down a phone number or save a quick photo, it's still useful. But when your note-taking system is also your work system, you need something that understands what you're doing.

Fromly doesn't ask you to change how you think. It adapts to how you already write. And when you have hundreds of notes and active projects, that makes all the difference.
