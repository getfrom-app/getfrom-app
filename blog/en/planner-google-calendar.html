Planner + Google Calendar: your tasks and events in one timeline

Drag a task to a time slot. Google Calendar events appear alongside. One view, zero friction.

From Blog · 5 junio 2026 · 5 min read

Most productivity setups require you to live in two places at once: a task manager for your to-dos, and a calendar for your meetings. You spend more time reconciling the two than actually working. From eliminates that gap.

With the Planner connected to Google Calendar, your tasks and events share a single timeline. You see exactly what you've committed to and what you need to do — without switching apps, copying events, or doing mental math about where your time went.

The problem with split systems

Here's a typical scenario: you have a 10am call, a 2pm review, and somewhere in between you need to finish a deliverable, respond to three people, and prep for tomorrow. Your calendar shows the calls. Your task list shows the rest. Neither tells you whether the day is actually feasible.

The result is constant context-switching. You check the calendar, switch to tasks, try to mentally slot things in, and end up either over-committing or forgetting something important. The system doesn't reflect reality — you're managing two separate models of your day.

From treats time as structure, not metadata. When you drag a task to a time block, you're not adding a due date — you're making a commitment on your actual schedule.

How From's Planner works

The Planner is a time-based view inside From. It displays your day in slots, and your Google Calendar events appear directly inside it — no import, no sync delay. When a meeting lands on your calendar, From already knows about it.

The Planner shows your time blocks and calendar events side by side in a single view.

On top of that, any task you've created in From — whether it's a simple to-do, a node inside a project, or something you captured in a quick note — can be dragged directly onto a time slot. The moment you do, From knows you've scheduled that task for that time. No due date field. No form to fill out. Just drag.

What gets shown in the Planner

  • Google Calendar events: All your connected calendars appear in the timeline automatically. Meetings, reminders, personal blocks — everything.
  • Scheduled tasks: Any task you drag to a slot becomes a time block on your Planner.
  • Today's note: The daily note surfaces alongside your schedule so context is always at hand.

Using the Planner in practice

Start your morning in the Planner. You'll immediately see your meetings for the day — the 9:30 standup, the 12:00 lunch block, the 4pm client call. Between those events are open slots. That's your real available time.

Now pull up your task list and start dragging. The deliverable that needs to go out before noon? Drag it to the 10:30 slot. The email you've been putting off? Slot it at 11:15. You're not estimating — you're deciding.

Tasks sit next to calendar events. Drag to schedule. The day becomes concrete.

From doesn't require you to set durations manually. You decide how much space each task takes by how you place it. The visual layout gives you immediate feedback: if you've stacked five tasks into two hours of real time, you'll see it — and you can adjust before the day starts.

Working with calendar events

Calendar events from Google appear as blocks in the timeline. From reads them as real commitments — not decorations. If you have a 90-minute meeting, that space is visually occupied and From treats it as unavailable for task placement.

This matters because From understands your actual capacity. It's not showing you a list and hoping you do the math. The Planner makes the math visual and immediate.

Tasks that move with you

If something doesn't get done, you don't lose it. Unfinished tasks stay in your list. You can drag them to tomorrow's Planner, reschedule them, or leave them pending — From keeps track without requiring you to manually clean up a schedule that didn't survive contact with reality.

One view is enough. You don't need a separate app for scheduling. The Planner is where your notes, tasks, and calendar converge — and From keeps them in sync automatically.

Setting it up

  1. Connect your Google account from From's settings. Calendar events will appear in the Planner immediately.
  2. Open the Planner from the sidebar or the keyboard shortcut.
  3. Find a task in your list or in any note — From recognizes tasks anywhere in your outline.
  4. Drag the task to the time slot where you want to work on it.
  5. Repeat until your available time is accounted for.

That's the full setup. No configuration files. No integration layers. From detects your calendar, understands your tasks, and gives you one place to plan.

The result

Planning your day stops being a chore and becomes a two-minute ritual. You open the Planner, see what's fixed, fill the gaps with what matters, and start. From handles the connection between your commitments and your intentions — so you don't have to.

Stop managing two systems. Let From hold both, and get back to doing the actual work.
