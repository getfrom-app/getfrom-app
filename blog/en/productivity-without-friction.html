What friction means in productivity

Friction is any obstacle between intention and action. In a productivity system, friction appears when: you spend time figuring out where to save something, the system requires configuration before you can use it, you have to switch apps to complete an action, or the process has more steps than necessary.

Every friction point reduces the likelihood that you'll use the system. And a system you don't use doesn't make you more productive — it makes you exactly as productive as having no system, but with the frustration of having spent time setting it up.

Fromly is designed from the ground up to eliminate friction. It's not a secondary goal — it's the main design principle.

Frictionless capture in Fromly

In Fromly, capturing something takes a minimum path:

  1. Press H to go to today's note
  2. Write what you want to capture
  3. Press Enter to add the next line

Three steps. You eliminate the first if you're already on today's note. The third disappears if you only need one line. In practice, capturing something in Fromly can be as fast as 2 seconds.

You don't have to decide which folder it goes in, you don't have to open a different app for tasks vs. notes, you don't have to format anything. You just write.

The keyboard shortcuts that change everything

Keyboard shortcuts are the primary friction-reduction tool. Memorizing them takes a few days — using them becomes automatic within a week:

  • H — Go to today's note
  • N — New node in the current context
  • Cmd+Enter — Convert node to task with checkbox
  • Cmd+F — Open search filters
  • P — Open/close planner
  • Space — Open Magic AI
  • Tab — Indent node (make it a child)
  • Shift+Tab — Outdent node (move up one level)

With these 8 shortcuts memorized, you can manage your entire system without touching the mouse. Work speed multiplies because there are no interruptions to hunt for buttons or menus.

⌨️ Exercise: For one week, force yourself to use only the keyboard in Fromly. By the end of the week, the shortcuts are automatic reflexes and your work speed will have increased noticeably.

Voice capture: zero friction

There are moments where even the keyboard has friction: when you're walking, when your hands are occupied, when an idea surfaces in the middle of a conversation. For those moments, voice capture eliminates all friction.

In Fromly, hold R and speak. When you release, the transcription appears in the Magic AI field and you can send it directly. The AI creates the node in the right place in your tree.

On iPhone, voice capture works the same way — hold the microphone button and speak. From the lock screen if you have the widget configured.

Frictionless retrieval: smart filters

Friction doesn't only appear in capture — it also appears in retrieval. If finding something in your system takes more than 30 seconds, you stop searching in it. And if you don't search, the system loses all its value.

Fromly has filters that accept natural language. You don't have to remember technical operators or know which node something was saved in. Write what you're looking for and it appears.

The most frequently used filters can be saved as panels in the sidebar — one click to see them. No additional friction.

Building low-friction habits

The ultimate goal isn't a perfect system — it's a system you use without thinking about it. Low-friction habits are those that don't require willpower to execute because the system does the heavy lifting.

With Fromly, these are the three habits that have the most impact with the least friction:

  1. Daily capture — Every time an idea or task comes up, write it in Fromly immediately. No processing, no deciding where it goes. Just capture.
  2. Morning review — Every morning, 5 minutes looking at "Pending" and "Today". Define the 3 most important things for the day.
  3. Day close — Every evening, 2 minutes marking what you completed. This activates the dopamine of progress and reinforces the habit.
