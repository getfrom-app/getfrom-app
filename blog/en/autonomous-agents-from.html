Autonomous agents: From works while you sleep

Schedule an agent at 08:00 daily. It reads your tree, runs the task and writes the result in your diary.

From Blog · 5 junio 2026 · 5 min read

The problem with manual review

Every morning, most people open their task manager and spend 10–15 minutes doing the same thing: scanning what's open, checking what's due, and figuring out what matters. It's not thinking — it's maintenance. Low-value, repetitive, and completely automatable.

The issue isn't discipline or focus. The issue is that most tools are passive. They wait for you. From isn't.

What autonomous agents actually do

From lets you schedule agents — small, targeted routines that run at a specific time, read your tree, execute a defined task, and write the result directly into your diary. No manual trigger. No dashboard to check. The work happens whether you're at your desk or not.

This isn't a generic automation pipeline. From's agents understand the structure of your notes — they know what's a task, what's a context, what's pinned, what's pending. When an agent reads your tree, it doesn't parse raw text. It understands intent.

Key idea: An agent scheduled at 08:00 runs before you open your laptop. By the time you sit down, your diary already has a summary of what needs your attention today.

From reads your tree with context — not just keywords

A concrete example: the daily briefing agent

Here's a real setup you can replicate in minutes:

  1. Create a node called Daily Briefing Agent at the root of your tree.
  2. Inside, write the instruction: Every morning at 08:00, review all open tasks due today or overdue, check any flagged notes from yesterday, and write a structured summary in today's diary entry.
  3. Schedule it to run daily at 08:00.

When the agent runs, it scans your entire tree, identifies what's pending, checks dates, and produces a clean entry in your daily note. You open From and the briefing is already there — tasks sorted by priority, a note on anything overdue, and a clean list of what to focus on.

No clicking around. No manual filtering. The agent did the maintenance so you can do the thinking.

Beyond task review: what else agents handle

Daily briefings are just the starting point. From agents can handle a range of recurring routines:

  • Weekly capture review: Every Sunday at 18:00, review all unprocessed nodes captured during the week and flag anything that needs a decision.
  • Project status check: Every Friday, scan a specific context (a project, a client, a goal) and write a brief status update in the diary.
  • Stale node detection: Daily, identify any note or task that hasn't been touched in more than 7 days and surface it for review.
  • Deadline proximity alerts: Each morning, list any node with a date within the next 48 hours and write it prominently in today's entry.

Each of these runs automatically. Each writes its output into your diary, creating a living log of what your system has done on your behalf.

The diary becomes a record of both your work and your agents' work

How to set up your first agent

Setting up an agent in From takes less than two minutes:

  1. Open From and navigate to the node or context you want the agent to work on.
  2. Use the contextual menu to create a scheduled agent on that node.
  3. Write a clear, specific instruction — what to read, what to do, where to write the result.
  4. Set the schedule: time, frequency (daily, weekly, weekdays only).
  5. Save. That's it.

The instruction you write doesn't need to follow a rigid format. From understands natural language and maps it to your actual tree structure. If you say "review overdue tasks in the Work context," From knows where Work is, what tasks live there, and what overdue means relative to today's date.

Tip: Start with one agent. A daily 08:00 briefing that lists today's tasks. Run it for a week. Once you trust it, add a second routine. Build your automated layer incrementally.

The result: a system that runs itself

The goal of autonomous agents isn't to replace your judgment — it's to eliminate the repetitive work that consumes your attention before you even start. When From handles the daily scan, the weekly review, and the deadline check, you arrive at your work with context already loaded.

Your diary becomes more than a log. It becomes a record of decisions made, tasks surfaced, and routines executed — with or without you. That's what a system that actually works looks like.
