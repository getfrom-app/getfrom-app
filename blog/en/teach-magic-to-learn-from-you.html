Teach Magic: how Fromly learns from your corrections

Right-click any node → Teach Magic. Fromly applies it from that moment on.

Fromly Blog · 5 junio 2026 · 5 min read

The problem with tools that don't adapt

Every productivity app has its own logic. Its own way of interpreting what you write, what it considers a task versus a note, what it treats as urgent versus someday. And most of the time, that logic doesn't match yours.

You write "call the accountant" and the app does nothing. You write "→ send invoice" and it doesn't know if that's a task or a reminder. You end up bending your language to fit the tool instead of the tool bending to fit you.

Fromly works differently. And Teach Magic is the feature that makes that difference concrete.

What Teach Magic actually does

Teach Magic is a correction mechanism. When Fromly interprets a node in a way you don't agree with — wrong type, wrong date, wrong classification — you right-click and select Teach Magic. You tell Fromly what that node should be. Fromly applies the correction immediately and remembers it.

From that moment on, similar nodes get interpreted the same way you showed it. Not through a settings panel. Not through a rules editor. Just by correcting it once, naturally, in context.

Right-click any node to access Teach Magic directly from the context menu.

This is not about training a generic model. This is Fromly learning your patterns — the way you phrase things, the shortcuts you use, the structures that make sense to your brain.

A concrete example

Say you always write your tasks with a dash and a verb: - review proposal, - send contract, - call client. Fromly picks most of them up correctly. But one day you write - check numbers and Fromly treats it as a plain note instead of a task.

You right-click. Teach Magic. You mark it as a task. Done.

Next time you write - check something, Fromly already knows. It classifies it correctly without you having to do anything. The correction propagates forward.

Why this matters more than you think

Most tools give you configuration options. You go into settings, define rules, set up filters, map your terminology to their terminology. It takes time, it feels like work, and it still doesn't cover every edge case.

Teach Magic flips that. The configuration happens in the moment, at the point of friction, without leaving your flow. You see something wrong, you fix it, Fromly learns. The overhead is near zero.

The key insight: Fromly doesn't ask you to adapt upfront. It adapts to you over time, correction by correction, until its understanding of your language matches yours.

This is especially powerful for people who use non-standard phrasing. If you write tasks in Spanish but dates in English. If you use symbols to mean specific things. If your shorthand doesn't follow any convention. Fromly can learn all of it — because you're teaching it directly, not hoping it figures it out from a manual.

How to use it: step by step

  1. Write normally. Don't adjust your language to match what you think Fromly expects. Write the way you think.
  2. Notice when something's off. Wrong node type, wrong classification, missing date detection — anything that doesn't match your intent.
  3. Right-click the node. Select Teach Magic from the context menu.
  4. Set the correct interpretation. Tell Fromly what that node actually is.
  5. Move on. Fromly applies it immediately and carries the learning forward.

Fromly shows you how it interprets each node — Teach Magic lets you correct that interpretation directly.

There's no batch process, no training session, no configuration wizard. Each correction is a single action that takes two seconds. Over days and weeks, those corrections accumulate into a version of Fromly that understands your specific way of working.

What Fromly detects — and can learn to detect better

Fromly already handles a lot out of the box. It detects tasks, notes, events, and resources. It picks up dates and times from natural language. It understands hierarchy from indentation and structure.

But your edge cases are yours. The way you write a recurring commitment. The abbreviation you use for a specific project. The format you've used for years that no app has ever understood correctly. Teach Magic is the bridge between Fromly's defaults and your actual patterns.

  • A node written as a question that you always mean as a task
  • A date format you prefer that differs from the standard
  • A specific phrase that always means a specific type of event
  • A resource link pattern that Fromly doesn't recognize automatically

All of these are teachable. All of them become part of how Fromly works for you specifically.

The result: less friction, more flow

The goal of Teach Magic isn't to make Fromly perfect on day one. It's to make Fromly get better every day you use it, without extra work on your part. Each correction is invisible overhead that pays forward indefinitely. Within a few weeks of normal use, Fromly stops surprising you with wrong interpretations — because it's had enough corrections to understand your language.

Write the way you think. Correct what's wrong when you see it. Fromly handles the rest.
